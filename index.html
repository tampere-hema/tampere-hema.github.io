TAMPERE HEMA

HISTORICAL EUROPEAN MARTIAL ARTS

WHO WE ARE

Tampere HEMA (Tampereen historiallisen miekkailun seura ry) is a historical European martial arts club in Tampere. We hold weekly training sessions in two main systems: Armizare (Longsword, Dagger, Wrestling) and I.33 (Sword and Buckler). Longsword, being the most popular weapon, takes up the most training hours each week. Training is typically conducted in English, but if all participants are Finnish speakers, the session will be held in Finnish.

FIORE DE’I LIBERI

Fiore de’i Liberi was an Italian fencing master born around 1350. He is best known for writing The Flower of Battle (Fior di Battaglia), one of the oldest surviving martial arts treatises in Europe. Having trained in the noble art of combat for decades, Fiore compiled his knowledge into a structured system, emphasizing both armed and unarmed techniques.

ARMIZARE

Armizare is one of the names Fiore used to describe his system of combat, which he taught to knights and noblemen. It includes the arts of fighting with longsword, dagger, spear, and poleaxe, as well as grappling and mounted combat. Armizare was designed for fights both in tourneys and in lethal contexts. The manuscripts detail and illustrate many techniques, but they also highlight the virtues a fencer should acquire and muster: Prudence, Celerity, Courage and Strength.

THE I.33 MANUSCRIPT

The Oldest Known Fencing Manual

The I.33 manuscript, also called the Walpurgis Fechtbuch, is the oldest known European fencing manual, dating to around 1320. Written in Latin, it depicts a fencing system focused on the sword and buckler. The manuscript features a priest instructing a student, presenting techniques through a series of illustrated plays with accompanying text.

I.33 emphasizes fencing from the bind and active buckler use. It’s a challenging manuscript to work with, but it offers a lot of room for different interpretations.

COMPETING

The Sport Side of HEMA

If you're looking to challenge yourself, there are several open tournaments held across Finland throughout the year. We encourage participation, and some of our members regularly compete in these events.
Most tournaments focus on longsword, but sometimes other weapons are included as well. Competitions are a great way to test your skills, meet fencers from other clubs, and experience the wider community.
That said—competing is totally optional. While many of us enjoy the thrill of competition, it's perfectly fine if tournaments aren’t your thing. Taking part is encouraged—but never required.

CLUB RULES

  1. Safety comes first

    Every club member is responsible for their safety and the safety of their training partners. During training, the instructions and advice from the instructor must be followed. You are entitled to raise your concerns regarding your safety or the safety of your training partners.

  2. Use gear appropriate for the training and in good condition

    Follow the gear requirements from the instructor for each exercise. Weapons used for free play and sparring must be designed for sparring (flexible, safe tip). Blunt training weapons are still dangerous and should be treated with appropriate respect and caution at all times.

  3. Report incidents and injuries immediately to the instructor

    A first aid kit is available on training premises.

  4. Turn up to training in time, fit and ready to take part

    You must never train under the influence of alcohol, drugs or any substance which could hinder your physical or mental abilities. Wear appropriate clothing for physical activity. Never wear jewelry, rings or necklace during training.

  5. Train at your own rhythm

    If an exercise feels uncomfortable or you need to rest, take a break and continue when you feel ready. You can ask the instructor for help to adapt an exercise to your own body limitations. If you have an injury or a medical condition which could hinder your training, inform the instructor at the start of the class.

  6. Use only the appropriate amount of force

    Your speed and intensity must always remain safe, controlled and adapted to the amount of protective equipment worn, to the skill level of both fencers and to the type of training. You must decrease your intensity if your training partner asks you to.

  7. Treat every club member with dignity, respect and consideration

    Discrimination, bullying or harassment are unacceptable. If you witness or are a victim of such behavior, please speak to the instructor or a member of the board.

  8. Follow good sportsmanship

    Never discourage an honest effort. HEMA, as a martial art, is inherently dangerous : it should be practiced in a collaborative way. Visible cues such as salutes are used to confirm readiness before starting a drill, free play or sparring.
