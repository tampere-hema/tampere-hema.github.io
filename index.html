Fiore

Fiore de’ Liberi was an Italian fencing master born around 1350. He is best known for writing The Flower of Battle (Fior di Battaglia), one of the oldest surviving martial arts treatises in Europe. Having trained in the noble art of combat for decades, Fiore compiled his knowledge into a structured system of medieval Italian martial arts, emphasizing both armed and unarmed techniques.

Armizare

Armizare is the name Fiore used to describe his system of combat, which encompasses a complete martial discipline for knights and men-at-arms. It includes techniques for fighting with longswords, daggers, spears, and poleaxes, as well as grappling and mounted combat. Armizare is designed for both duels and battlefield engagements, blending strategy, physical skill, and tactical principles.

The I.33 Manuscript – The Oldest Known Fencing Manual

The I.33 manuscript, also called the Walpurgis Fechtbuch, is the oldest known European fencing manual, dating to around 1320. Written in Latin, it depicts a fencing system focused on the sword and buckler. The manuscript features a priest instructing a student, presenting techniques through a series of illustrated plays with accompanying text.

Unlike later medieval manuals, I.33 emphasizes fluid, adaptive movement and complex defensive structures. It remains a vital resource for historical European martial arts (HEMA), offering unique insights into early medieval combat.
